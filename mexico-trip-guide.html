Getting There: MCI → Mexico City

Flights from MCI

Search Kansas City (MCI) to Mexico City (MEX) flights early and compare nonstop and one-stop options — schedules and availability shift as June 2026 approaches. Prices spike on match weekends.

Best Travel Dates

Arrive June 9 or 10 to settle in before the June 11 opener. Stay through June 25 to catch all three group-stage matches without a rush departure.

At the Airport

Land at AICM Terminal 1 or 2. Use the authorized taxi booths inside arrivals or open Uber before stepping outside — never accept unsolicited ride offers inside the terminal.

Where to Stay

Roma Norte

Trendy, walkable, packed with incredible restaurants and nightlife. Excellent metro access to the rest of the city.

Condesa

Beautiful tree-lined streets, very safe, peaceful bohemian vibe. Located right next door to Reforma.

Polanco

Upscale, elite security, located right near Chapultepec Park. The absolute best option for luxury stays.

Hotel tip: Rooms in Roma Norte, Condesa, and Polanco book up quickly around major match dates. Search both cities filtered to your World Cup travel dates.

Getting to Estadio Azteca

  1. Step 1 · Metro Line 2 (Blue): Board at your nearest central station and ride south to the terminal station, Tasqueña.
  2. Step 2 · Tren Ligero Transfer: Follow the signs at Tasqueña to connect to the Tren Ligero (Light Rail) and ride to the Estadio Azteca stop.
  3. Total Travel Time: Plan for 45–75 minutes from central CDMX.
Match Day Pro-Tips: Buy an Integrated Mobility Card at any metro station ahead of time. On match days, leave 3–4 hours early. Check official FIFA, host city, and stadium transport updates before match day for the latest shuttle and transit plans. If you need a backup, ride-sharing apps like Uber or DiDi are your best option.

Full Trip Itinerary

  • June 9–10: Arrive in Mexico City · Rest up, check into your hotel, explore Roma/Condesa, and eat authentic street tacos.
  • June 11 · MATCH DAY (Mexico vs. South Africa): Leave early for the stadium · Soak up the Estadio Azteca Fan Zone atmosphere before kickoff.
  • June 12–16: Teotihuacan day trip (sunrise hot air balloon!) · Visit the Zócalo Fan Festival · Explore historic city sights.
  • June 17–19: Head to Guadalajara for Mexico vs. South Korea · 1-hour domestic flight or ~5-hour express bus.
  • June 23–25 · Final Group Match: Return to CDMX for Czechia vs. Mexico at Azteca · Celebrate at the Fan Festivals · Fly home to Kansas City.

Teotihuacan Day Trip

🎈 Bucket List Moment: Hot Air Balloon at Sunrise

Pickup is usually around 4:00–5:00 AM. Experience a breathtaking flight over the ancient pyramids at dawn, followed by an optional walking visit to the archaeological site. Costs average $150–$270 including transportation and breakfast. Spots fill up weeks out — book early.

Hours & Entry

Open 9 AM – 5 PM. Entry is roughly $4–5 USD. Arrive at opening to beat the tour groups.

Best Way to Go

Book a guided tour with hotel pickup ($50–$120) or take an affordable public bus from Autobuses del Norte station.

Pyramid Climbing Note

Full summits of the Pyramid of the Sun are restricted. Lower platforms and base steps remain open for great photos.

What to Pack

Sturdy walking shoes, high-SPF sunscreen, 2 liters of water, and a light rain jacket (June is the start of rainy season).

Staying Safe in Mexico City

Stick to tourist safe zones: Roma, Condesa, Polanco, Reforma, Zócalo.
Use Uber or DiDi apps only — never hail unmarked street taxis.
Lock your passport in the hotel safe; keep valuables hidden while walking.
Travel in groups or pairs, especially on match days.
CDMX is high altitude — hydrate constantly and pace your alcohol intake.
Emergency number is 911. Register your trip with the U.S. STEP program.
Download the official CDMX safety app and save Google Maps offline.
Security and crowd-control plans vary by match and city — follow official alerts and avoid informal transportation or street offers.
