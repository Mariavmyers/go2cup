World Cup Final 2026: Where to Stay & How to Get There

Everything a fan needs to plan the biggest match on the planet — airports, hotels, transfers, and the New York trip of a lifetime.

🏟 MetLife Stadium 📅 Sun, July 19, 2026 📍 East Rutherford, NJ

The 2026 World Cup Final lands at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey on Sunday, July 19 — the final whistle on the first 48-team, three-nation World Cup. If you're making the trip, here's how to lock in your stay, your flights, and your match-day plan before prices climb.

Getting to New York for the Final

The New York metro area has three major airports. Newark Liberty (EWR) is the closest to MetLife Stadium, but JFK and LaGuardia (LGA) both work well if you'd rather base yourself in the city. Book flights early — Final week is the single busiest travel window of the entire tournament.

Where to Stay

Two smart bases for the Final:

  • Manhattan — best for nightlife, restaurants, and sightseeing between the celebrations. You'll commute to the stadium on match day, but you're in the heart of the city.
  • Northern New Jersey (Secaucus, Rutherford, Jersey City) — closer to MetLife and often easier on the wallet, with a quick hop to the Meadowlands on match day.

Whichever you pick, book now. Final-week rooms sell out and rates spike the closer you get to July 19.

Match-Day Tips for MetLife

  • Arrive early. Security and crowds peak for the Final — give yourself extra time.
  • Trains from the city: NJ Transit runs from Penn Station to the Meadowlands on match day. Driving? Pre-book parking or a transfer.
  • Clear-bag policy applies at the stadium — travel light.
  • July heat: hydrate, and check the forecast before you head out.
Traveling light or got a delayed flight? A couple of extras worth knowing: luggage pickup & delivery to skip the hauling, and a flight-delay compensation check if your inbound got disrupted.

Final 2026 — Fan FAQ

Where is the 2026 World Cup Final being played?

Sunday, July 19, 2026 at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey — just across the river from New York City.

Which airport is closest to MetLife Stadium?

Newark Liberty (EWR) is closest. JFK and LaGuardia (LGA) also serve the metro area and connect to the stadium by transfer or train.

Where should I stay for the Final?

Manhattan for the city experience, or northern New Jersey (Secaucus, Rutherford, Jersey City) to stay closer to MetLife. Book early — Final-week demand is extreme.

How do I get from NYC to MetLife on match day?

NJ Transit runs trains from Penn Station to the Meadowlands. Many fans pre-book a private transfer or rental car to beat the crowds.
