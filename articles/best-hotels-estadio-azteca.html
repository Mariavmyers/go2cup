Best Hotels Near Estadio Azteca for World Cup 2026

May 21, 2026 · 6 min read · Maria Myers · GO2CUP · sportdgx.com

Most first-time visitors to Mexico City make the same mistake: they book a hotel in the tourist center — Zócalo, Centro Histórico, Reforma — and then discover that Estadio Azteca is an hour away on the other side of the city. The stadium sits in the far south of CDMX, in the borough of Tlalpan near the Coyoacán and Xochimilco areas. It is not close to where most tourists stay. This guide breaks down your real options so you can make the right choice before match day.

The good news: Mexico City's metro system is excellent, cheap, and goes directly to the stadium. Wherever you stay, you can get there. The question is how long it takes and what tradeoffs you're making.

Book Now — Prices Are Rising Fast

Mexico City hotel rooms — especially near the stadium and in Roma/Condesa — are already selling out for World Cup match dates. Prices near Estadio Azteca for the Opening Match (June 11) are already 3–4x normal rates. Don't wait.

Understanding the Geography

Mexico City is enormous — home to 9 million in the city proper and 21+ million in the metro area. Estadio Azteca sits in the far south. The tourist neighborhoods (Roma, Condesa, Polanco, Centro Histórico) are in the center-north of the city. Transit options to the stadium exist, but they take 45–70 minutes depending on your starting point and the line conditions.

Getting to Estadio Azteca: The Route
  • Take Metro Line 2 (blue line) to Tasqueña station — the southern terminus
  • Exit Metro and follow signs to the Tren Ligero (light rail) connection — same station complex
  • Ride the Tren Ligero south to Estadio Azteca station — the stop is named for the stadium
  • Walk approximately 5–8 minutes from the Tren Ligero exit to the stadium gates

Total time from Roma Norte: approximately 50–65 minutes. From Polanco: 60–75 minutes. From near Tasqueña: 15–20 minutes.

Option 1: Stay Near the Stadium

Near-Stadium Hotels
Easiest Match Access

There are limited hotel options in the immediate area around Estadio Azteca — this is a residential/commercial southern CDMX neighborhood, not a tourist district. Hotels here include the Courtyard by Marriott Perinorte (reachable from the southern highway corridor), as well as NH Collection properties accessible from the Tlalpan area. Business-oriented hotels further south along Insurgentes Sur are also viable options with quicker access to the Tren Ligero.

The key advantage: on match day, you are 10–15 minutes from the gates. The key disadvantage: you are not in a neighborhood with excellent food, nightlife, or sightseeing. If your trip is entirely about the football, this works. If you also want to explore Mexico City, you'll be far from the best of it.

Match-day transit: 10–20 min Nightlife/food: Limited Availability: Very limited — book immediately

Option 2: Stay in Roma / Condesa (Our Recommendation)

Roma Norte, Roma Sur, Condesa
Best Overall Base

Roma and Condesa are the heartbeat of modern Mexico City — tree-lined streets, outstanding restaurants, world-class coffee, vibrant nightlife, and a genuine neighborhood character that makes CDMX one of the best cities on earth to simply walk around and eat. Staying here gives you the full Mexico City experience outside of match days.

The tradeoff is transit time: expect 45–60 minutes to Estadio Azteca via Metro Line 2 to Tasqueña, then Tren Ligero to Estadio Azteca station. That's manageable — millions of chilangos commute longer daily. Buy a rechargeable metro card (Tarjeta de Movilidad Integrada), keep enough balance loaded, and enjoy the ride.

Match-day transit: 50–65 min Food & nightlife: Exceptional Price range: $80–$250 USD/night
Roma/Condesa Neighborhood Tips
  • Stay on or near Ámsterdam Avenue (Roma Norte) or Parque España (Condesa) for the best walkability
  • Mercado Medellín is the best local market for breakfast and lunch — go early
  • Bar and restaurant density here is extraordinary — you could eat world-class food three times a day without repeating
  • Rideshare apps (Uber, DiDi) work well in this area and are affordable by global standards

Option 3: Stay in Polanco (Luxury)

Polanco
Luxury Option

Polanco is Mexico City's upscale district — home to the best luxury hotels (St. Regis, Four Seasons, W Mexico City, Camino Real), embassies, high-end shopping, and fine dining including multiple Michelin-recognized restaurants. If budget is not the concern and you want maximum comfort and prestige, Polanco delivers.

Transit to the stadium from Polanco involves Metro Line 7 or Line 1 to Tasqueña connection points — expect 60–75 minutes total. Rideshare direct to the stadium from Polanco will cost significantly more but is available. Many luxury travelers in Polanco opt for private transfers on match day.

Match-day transit: 60–75 min (metro) or 30–45 min (rideshare) Price range: $200–$600+ USD/night

Booking Tips: What You Need to Know

  • Book immediately for Opening Match dates (June 11): Mexico vs South Africa at Estadio Azteca is the most in-demand match of the entire tournament in Mexico. Hotels within 5km of the stadium are already scarce at any reasonable price.
  • Midweek group stage matches have better availability than weekend and knockout games — flexibility on match dates matters.
  • Airbnb and short-term rentals in Roma and Condesa can be a better value than hotels — entire apartments in these neighborhoods at $80–$150/night give you a kitchen and more space.
  • Cancellation policies matter: Book refundable rates where possible until the group stage draw makes your specific match dates clear.
  • Altitude: Mexico City sits at 2,240 meters (7,350 feet). Fans coming from sea-level countries should budget a day of adjustment before heavy activity — light headaches and fatigue are common in the first 24–48 hours.
