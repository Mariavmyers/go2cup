SATURDAY AT
THE WORLD CUP:
SUPER-SUB UNDAV
STUNS IVORY COAST

The Netherlands ran riot in Houston, Germany staged a stunning comeback in Toronto with substitute Deniz Undav scoring twice, Curaçao earned a historic World Cup point in Kansas City, and Japan face Tunisia late in Monterrey.

June 20, 2026 · GO2CUP Editorial

Saturday, June 20, 2026
Netherlands 5–1 Sweden
Group F · Houston

The Oranje ran riot in Houston. Brian Brobbey and Cody Gakpo each scored a brace, and substitute Crysencio Summerville added a fifth in the 89th minute to complete a comprehensive demolition of Sweden. Anthony Elanga grabbed a late consolation for the Swedes, but there was no way back. The victory moves the Netherlands top of Group F on four points. The win also made the Netherlands just the 8th nation in World Cup history to reach 100 all-time World Cup goals.

★ Story of the Day
Germany 2–1 Ivory Coast
Group E · Toronto
Kessie 30’ (CIV) Undav 68’ (sub, equalizer) Undav 90+’ (sub, winner) Germany into Round of 32 First time since 2014 Undav: sub goals in both WC matches

It looked like Germany's tournament was about to take a very uncomfortable turn. Franck Kessie, the former Barcelona and AC Milan midfielder, put Ivory Coast ahead in the 30th minute and the four-time champions — who had already beaten Curaçao 7-1 in their opener — were staring at a result that would have complicated their path enormously in Toronto.

Then Deniz Undav came off the bench.

The Stuttgart striker had scored in Germany's opening rout but was named among the substitutes here. It didn't matter. Undav levelled in the 68th minute to silence the Ivory Coast end of the stadium, and then, deep into stoppage time, he drove home the winner to send the four-time champions into the Round of 32. Two goals from the bench. Two decisive moments. The kind of night that makes substitute appearances legendary.

The historical footnote is striking: Undav became one of only a handful of players in World Cup history to score both the tying and winning goals in the same match as a substitute. It is a feat that requires not just quality but nerve and timing — and Undav delivered both.

For Germany, the relief is palpable. They advance to the Round of 32 for the first time since 2014, when they went on to lift the trophy in Brazil. Whether this team has that kind of distance in them remains to be seen — but Undav's impact off the bench has given the coaching staff a weapon that every team in the tournament now has to plan for.

Ecuador 0–0 Curaçao
Group E · Kansas City

A goalless draw in Kansas City, but the story was all Curaçao goalkeeper Eloy Room, who made a string of outstanding saves to earn the Caribbean island nation a historic World Cup point. The smallest nation ever to reach a World Cup now has something to show for it. The result keeps Group E tightly contested ahead of the final round of matches.

⏲ Preview · Result Pending
Tunisia VS Japan
Group F · Monterrey · Late Saturday kickoff

Japan go into this one as strong favorites. The Samurai Blue have already shown they can handle the big occasion — their record against elite European sides at recent tournaments speaks for itself — and a win here would put them in an excellent position in Group F. Tunisia will need to be at their organized, disciplined best to spring an upset.
