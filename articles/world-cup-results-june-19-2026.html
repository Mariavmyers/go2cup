USA march on, Morocco edge Scotland in Boston, Brazil deliver a devastating three-goal first half against Haiti, and Paraguay squeeze past Türkiye in a Group D thriller.

Friday, June 19, 2026
USA 2–0 Australia
Group D

The co-hosts got the job done. A key goal before halftime put the United States in control and they never looked back, collecting three points that keep them moving toward the knockout rounds. The Socceroos, who shocked Türkiye in their opener, couldn't find a way through a resolute American backline.

Morocco 1–0 Scotland
Group C

Morocco won 1-0 at Boston Stadium, drawing level with Scotland on points and keeping Group C beautifully poised heading into the final matchday. The Tartan Army, who opened with a win over Haiti, now need a result against Brazil in Miami to advance.

★ Featured Match
Brazil 3–0 Haiti
Group C · Philadelphia Stadium
Cunha 23’ Cunha 36’ Vinicius Jr 45+2’ + assist on opener All 3 goals before halftime Brazil top Group C Haiti first nation eliminated Endrick: World Cup debut off bench

After a flat 1-1 opening draw with Morocco, the five-time world champions arrived in Philadelphia with a point to prove — and they made it emphatically, scoring all three goals before the break.

Carlo Ancelotti named Matheus Cunha in the starting lineup ahead of Igor Thiago, and the decision was vindicated almost immediately. Vinicius Junior found him in behind the Haitian defense in the 23rd minute and Cunha finished coolly to open the scoring. He struck again in the 36th, doubling Brazil's advantage with a clinical second. Then, in first-half stoppage time, Vinicius Junior added his name to the scoresheet himself — a goal and an assist in the first 45 minutes for the Madrid forward. Three goals. One half. The Brazil the world expects had finally shown up.

The result sends Haiti home as the first nation eliminated from World Cup 2026. They gave everything in both matches but couldn't cope with Brazil firing on all cylinders. For the five-time champions, it's three points, top spot in Group C, and momentum heading into the business end of the group stage.

There were notes of concern amid the celebration. Raphinha limped off during the second half with what appeared to be a hamstring injury — his availability for the knockout rounds is now in doubt. And teenager Endrick came off the bench to make his World Cup debut, giving Brazilian fans an early look at what comes next for the Seleção.

Next for Brazil: a group-deciding clash with Scotland in Miami. Win, and they march into the knockouts with a head of steam. For Scotland, still alive after their loss to Morocco, it is effectively a must-win.

Brazil’s next match vs Scotland · Miami · Group C decider
Paraguay 1–0 Türkiye
Group D

Paraguay edged Türkiye 1-0 in a tight Group D affair that had real knockout implications. La Albirroja collected three points to shape the standings ahead of the final round, leaving Group D unsettled going into the last set of matches.
