Hard Rock Stadium — Match Day HQ

Where Is Hard Rock Stadium — And How Do You Get There?

Hard Rock Stadium sits in Miami Gardens, about 18 miles north of South Beach and 8 miles from Miami International Airport. It is not downtown Miami — and that is important to know before you book your hotel. The stadium holds around 65,000 and is one of the premier NFL venues in the US, already confirmed as a 2026 FIFA World Cup venue hosting 7+ matches including knockout rounds.

The best hotel strategy is to either stay close to the stadium in Miami Gardens, or stay in a well-connected area like Downtown Miami or Brickell with easy transit or rideshare access. South Beach is beautiful but adds 40+ minutes each way on match days with traffic.

🚗 Getting to Hard Rock Stadium — Airport & Stadium Transfers

Airport Transfers & Stadium Transport

Miami International Airport (MIA) is about 8 miles from Hard Rock Stadium. Fort Lauderdale Airport (FLL) is about 20 miles north. On match days, rideshare demand spikes hard — pre-booking a private transfer eliminates stress and often costs the same as surge Uber.

🚐

Book a private transfer in advance to lock in your price before surge pricing hits on match day. Driver meets you at arrivals with a sign. No waiting, no rideshare queue, no surprises. Best for families, groups, and anyone with heavy luggage.

❓ Miami World Cup 2026 — Frequently Asked Questions

Miami Fan Travel FAQ

How far is Hard Rock Stadium from Miami International Airport? +
Hard Rock Stadium is approximately 8 miles (15–25 minute drive) from Miami International Airport (MIA). Fort Lauderdale Airport (FLL) is about 20 miles away, typically 25–35 minutes without traffic. Pre-book your transfer — on match days, rideshare queues at MIA can take 45+ minutes.
Should I stay near the stadium or downtown Miami? +
If your trip is primarily about football, stay in Miami Gardens near the stadium — zero transport stress on match days. If you want the full Miami city experience (beach, nightlife, Wynwood), stay in Brickell or Downtown Miami and budget 30–40 minutes each way on match days. South Beach is beautiful but adds unnecessary transport time.
What matches is Miami hosting at the 2026 World Cup? +
Miami's Hard Rock Stadium is confirmed to host 7+ FIFA World Cup 2026 matches, including Group Stage matches and at least one Knockout Round match. The full schedule with specific dates and teams will be confirmed after the tournament draw. Knockout round matches command 2–3x hotel premium vs group stage.
Do I need a visa to visit Miami for the World Cup? +
Most travelers from Europe, UK, Canada, Australia, and Japan can enter the USA under ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization) without a traditional visa — but ESTA must be applied for in advance online. Citizens of countries not covered by ESTA need a B-2 visitor visa. Check the US Department of State website and our Visa & Border guide for full details. FIFA will not process visas — you handle this independently.
Is Miami safe for international fans? +
Miami is generally safe for tourists in the areas most fans will visit — Brickell, Downtown, Miami Beach, and the stadium zone. Standard big-city precautions apply: watch your belongings, avoid poorly lit streets late at night, use official rideshare apps rather than approaching random drivers. The World Cup will have significant additional security presence around all venues and fan zones.
What is the weather like in Miami during the World Cup in June? +
Miami in June is hot and humid. Expect temperatures of 85–92°F (29–33°C) with high humidity. Afternoon thunderstorms are common — they pass quickly. Wear light, breathable clothing. Hard Rock Stadium is an open-air venue, so bring sunscreen, a hat, and water. The heat on match days is real — factor this into your planning.
How do I get from the stadium to South Beach after a match? +
After the match, rideshare demand spikes immediately. Your options: pre-book a return transfer with GetTransfer or Welcome Pickups before the match, take the Tri-Rail/Metrorail if your hotel is near a station, or walk to a designated rideshare pickup zone (further from the venue) where wait times are shorter. Allow 60–90 minutes for the full journey to South Beach after a night match.
