Arrowhead Stadium — Truman Sports Complex, KC

Where Is Arrowhead Stadium — And How Do You Plan Around It?

Arrowhead Stadium anchors the Truman Sports Complex on the eastern edge of the metro, flanked by Kauffman Stadium next door. The complex is purpose-built for big events but surrounded by surface parking rather than hotels or neighbourhoods — there is no stadium district to walk into before a match. Fans need a plan for getting in and out, whether that's a rideshare, a shuttle, or driving in with a parking pass.

The smartest approach: stay near the stadium itself along the I-70 corridor for the shortest match-day commute, or base yourself in Downtown KC for the best food, nightlife, and atmosphere between matches — with a 15–20 minute rideshare to Arrowhead on game days. Country Club Plaza and Midtown split the difference nicely.

🏨 Hotels Near Arrowhead Stadium — Kansas City World Cup 2026

Best Hotels Near Arrowhead Stadium for World Cup Fans

Kansas City's hotel market is well-priced compared to most World Cup host cities — but that advantage disappears fast on match dates. With a 76,000-seat stadium and one of the loudest fan bases in American sports, demand for match weekends will be intense. World Cup visitors should treat Arrowhead dates like Super Bowl weekends and book accordingly. Early bookings here genuinely pay off.
