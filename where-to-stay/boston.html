📍 Where to Stay — Boston World Cup 2026

Best Areas to Stay for Gillette Stadium

The Boston area gives you a real choice: stay close to the stadium in Foxborough for maximum match-day convenience, or base yourself in the city and factor in the commute. Both approaches work — but each comes with different trade-offs on price, nightlife, and travel time. Here's what you need to know.

🏟
Near Gillette Stadium / Foxborough
0–5 miles from stadium · Walk or short drive
The no-stress option. Foxborough and nearby Wrentham / Walpole have a strong cluster of mid-range and business hotels built around Patriot Place — the entertainment complex attached to Gillette. No traffic anxiety, no surge-priced Ubers, no 90-minute post-match crawl back to the city. Wrentham Premium Outlets nearby is a bonus for non-match days. The practical choice for fans attending multiple games.
Best Match-Day Convenience
🦞
Back Bay / Downtown Boston
~28 miles from stadium · 45–90 min by road or commuter rail
The full Boston experience — Newbury Street, the Freedom Trail, Fenway Park, world-class seafood, and a dense hotel supply across every price point. The MBTA Commuter Rail (Providence Line) runs a match-day service direct to Gillette Stadium — one of the most useful transit options of any 2026 host city. Stay in Back Bay, take the train to kick-off, and return to one of America's great walking cities. Plan travel times carefully on match days.
Best City Experience
🎓
Cambridge / Somerville
~30 miles from stadium · 50–90 min by road
Across the Charles River from downtown Boston — Harvard Square, MIT, a thriving independent restaurant scene, and some of the best value hotels in the metro area. Cambridge attracts a younger, more international crowd and the neighbourhoods around Inman Square and Porter Square are excellent for pre- and post-match eating and drinking. Slightly further from Gillette than Back Bay but often meaningfully cheaper, especially for multi-night stays.
