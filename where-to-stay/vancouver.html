📍 Where to Stay — Vancouver World Cup 2026

Best Areas to Stay for BC Place

Vancouver is one of the most walkable World Cup host cities on the entire 2026 roster — BC Place is centrally located and well-connected by SkyTrain. Your neighbourhood choice shapes your nightlife access, daily budget, and how far you are from the stadium gates.

🏟
Downtown Vancouver / BC Place Area
0–10 min walk to BC Place
The most obvious and most rewarding base — several major hotels sit within a 10-minute walk of the stadium gates. Downtown Vancouver has everything: waterfront views, Robson Street dining, Gastown nightlife, and the Stanley Park seawall. The premium location commands premium prices, especially on match dates. Book the moment you confirm your tickets — this zone will sell out completely.
Best Walkability
🍷
Yaletown
10–15 min walk · or 5 min SkyTrain
Vancouver's chicest neighbourhood — converted warehouse district now packed with upscale restaurants, wine bars, boutique hotels, and a lively but refined nightlife scene. A short walk or one SkyTrain stop from BC Place. Slightly more residential feel than central downtown, but with better restaurant density. Perfect for fans who want post-match dining and nightlife at a high standard without the Times Square-style tourist hustle.
Best Nightlife & Dining
✈️
Richmond
~25 min by SkyTrain (Canada Line)
The budget-smart play for Vancouver. Richmond sits directly on the Canada Line SkyTrain to downtown — 25 minutes, no transfers, no traffic anxiety. Hotel prices are 30–50% cheaper than equivalent downtown properties, and Richmond's food scene (particularly the Asian dining strip along No. 3 Road) is world-class in its own right. Ideal for fans on a tighter budget, flying in or out of YVR, or attending multiple matches.
Best Value
