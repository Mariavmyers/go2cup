The New York / New Jersey metro area gives you options that no other host city can — from stadium-adjacent New Jersey hotels to Midtown Manhattan towers and the waterfront neighbourhoods of Jersey City and Hoboken. Midtown Manhattan
~8 miles from stadium · 25–35 min NJ Transit from Penn Station
The New York City experience in full — Times Square, Central Park, world-class dining on every block, and the electric energy of the world's most famous skyline during a World Cup Final week. NJ Transit runs direct to MetLife Stadium from Penn Station, making match-day travel manageable. The most expensive zone by a wide margin, but for the Final, many fans will pay the premium to say they stayed in Manhattan. Book 12+ months out for any chance of reasonable rates.

🌊
Jersey City / Hoboken
~10 miles from stadium · 30–40 min via NJ Transit or PATH
The smart middle ground — waterfront hotels with Manhattan skyline views, significantly lower nightly rates than Midtown, and excellent transit in every direction. The PATH train connects Jersey City and Hoboken directly into lower Manhattan and midtown in minutes, while NJ Transit runs west toward the Meadowlands. Hoboken's Washington Street bar scene is one of the best in the metro area. For Final week, this zone offers the best value-to-experience ratio in the entire region.
