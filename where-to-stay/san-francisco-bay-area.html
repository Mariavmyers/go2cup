📍 Where to Stay — SF Bay Area World Cup 2026

Best Areas to Stay for Levi's Stadium

The Bay Area is enormous and the stadium sits squarely in Silicon Valley. Your neighbourhood choice has a direct impact on match-day travel time, nightly cost, and overall experience. These three zones cover the full range — from stadium-door convenience to the full San Francisco tourist experience.

🏟
Near Levi's Stadium / Santa Clara
0–3 miles from stadium · Walk or free shuttle
The smartest base for fans focused purely on match days. Santa Clara and neighbouring Sunnyvale have a strong mid-range and business hotel supply built for Silicon Valley conferences — clean, reliable, and well-priced compared to SF. Many properties are within shuttle or walking distance of the stadium. No traffic nightmares, no 90-minute Ubers home after the final whistle.
Best Match-Day Convenience
🌆
San Jose / Santana Row
~10 miles from stadium · 15–25 min by Uber
San Jose is the Bay Area's most underrated base for World Cup fans. Santana Row delivers upscale dining, rooftop bars, and genuine nightlife — think Beverly Hills-lite vibes in the middle of Silicon Valley. Downtown San Jose has strong hotel supply across all budgets. Light Rail connects to Santa Clara, and Uber to the stadium is a short, reliable run even on match days.
Best Balance of Value & Nightlife
🌉
San Francisco Proper
~45 miles from stadium · 60–90 min each way
The iconic choice — Golden Gate Bridge, Fisherman's Wharf, world-class food, and the full SF experience. But the commute to Levi's Stadium is genuinely long and Bay Area hotel rates are punishing. Best for fans blending the World Cup with a broader SF holiday. If you go this route, plan match-day logistics carefully — rideshare surge pricing post-match can be brutal.
Best Tourist Experience
