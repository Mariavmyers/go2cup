📍 Where to Stay — Atlanta World Cup 2026

Best Areas to Stay for Mercedes-Benz Stadium

Atlanta's MARTA rail system connects Downtown, Midtown, and points beyond — making this one of the more transit-friendly U.S. host cities. Still, your neighbourhood choice determines nightly cost, nightlife access, and walkability on match days. These three zones cover every type of fan.

🏟
Downtown Atlanta / Stadium District
0–10 min walk · or 5 min MARTA
The most convenient base in the city — several major hotels are within a 10-minute walk of the stadium gates. Centennial Olympic Park, the Georgia Aquarium, and CNN Center are all walkable from here. Prices are elevated but the logistics are unbeatable. For fans attending multiple matches or arriving late, walking back from a night game beats any Uber queue in the city. Book before match schedules drop — this zone disappears first.
Best Match-Day Walkability
🌳
Midtown Atlanta
~2 miles from stadium · 15 min MARTA or Uber
Atlanta's most liveable and culturally rich neighbourhood — Piedmont Park, the High Museum of Art, brilliant restaurant rows on Peachtree Street, and a lively bar scene. MARTA's Red and Gold lines connect directly to the stadium zone in minutes. Mid-range and boutique hotel supply is excellent. The sweet spot for fans who want genuine Atlanta character alongside effortless match-day access. Best All-Round Experience
💎
Buckhead
~8 miles from stadium · 25–35 min by MARTA or Uber
Atlanta's upscale northern district — luxury hotels, high-end dining along Peachtree Road, Lenox Square shopping, and the city's most polished nightlife scene. MARTA's Gold Line runs directly from Buckhead to the stadium, making match-day travel manageable even from this distance. The premium choice for fans prioritising five-star comfort and Atlanta's finest restaurants over proximity to the gate.
