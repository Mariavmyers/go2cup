Accessibility Statement

Last Updated: May 10, 2026

MainEntry LLC is committed to making GO2CUP accessible to as many people as possible, including those with disabilities. We strive to meet or exceed applicable accessibility standards.

Our Commitment

We aim to follow the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 published by the W3C Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI) at Level AA. These guidelines help make web content more accessible to people with disabilities, including visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, language, learning, and neurological disabilities.

What We're Doing

  • Using semantic HTML structure for screen reader compatibility
  • Providing meaningful alt text for images where applicable
  • Maintaining sufficient color contrast across page elements
  • Designing pages to be navigable via keyboard
  • Using legible, appropriately sized typography
  • Avoiding flashing content that may trigger seizures

Known Limitations

While we strive for full WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, some third-party content, embedded tools, or legacy pages may not yet meet all standards. We are working to improve accessibility across all GO2CUP pages on an ongoing basis.

Accessibility for World Cup Fans

We recognize that World Cup fans include people of all abilities. We are committed to providing accessible travel, venue, and event information for fans with disabilities where possible.

Feedback & Contact

If you experience accessibility barriers on GO2CUP, please contact us. We take all accessibility feedback seriously and will work to address issues.
